openCurrents is a currency generated by social action.

Give 1 hour to a social organization to create and receive 1 Current.

Suggested value: 1 = $10

Why it's going to work

Advantage #1: Available to all

For any currency to become a viable alternative to Old Money, widespread adoption is critical.

Other currencies such as Bitcoin have been too technical and cumbersome for most people to adopt.

openCurrents makes it easy to earn currency, find buyers and sellers, and exchange. "Yes, I accept openCurrents." It's that easy.

Advantage #2

Advantage #2: Global scope

Our world is more interconnected than ever. Whether we’re using dollars or pesos or euros, it’s all the Same Old System (SOS).

Many currencies focus on supporting local economy. While this is a worthy cause, it does not create a viable alternative to Old Money.

openCurrents aims to become available to everyone, everywhere.

Advantage #3

Advantage #3: Timing

The World Wide Web was born on August 6th, 1991. We have barely scratched the surface of what we are collectively capable of. Discontent with the status quo is as great as it’s ever been.

Nothing is more powerful than an idea whose time has come.

The time has come. Are you ready?

What about Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a stepping stone

Bitcoin has proven that virtual currency can have real value. However, its design has limitations that will never allow it to become a viable alternative to Old Money.

Since Bitcoin is anonymous, it cannot offer the same security that a bank can provide. If someone steals your key, your Bitcoin is lost because you can't prove the account was yours in the first place.

Furthermore, Bitcoin's system of currency creation is too technical for most people to participate in. Computer programmers effectively become gatekeepers to the currency.

openCurrents verifies your identity to guarantee your funds. Since everyone has time to give, everyone can create Currents.

Why do we need this?

Old Money Problems

The world's money system continues to get worse at its only job - helping people exchange. Two reasons:

Debt. Money is created as debt - banks do nothing to earn the money that is printed and given to them. Not only is it impossible to repay this "loan", but in 2009 the US government gave banks $900 billion of taxpayer money to keep this sinking system afloat.

Gatekeepers. Banks are the bottleneck, deciding who gets access to money. Gatekeepers have become less influential in other industries with the rise of technology, but government has obstructed the natural evolution of currency - until now.

Currents are created through time and effort, not through debt. Anyone can create it, so there are no gatekeepers.
